CSE 167: Intro to Computer Graphics

Surface of Revolution Editor

Fall 2013

This purpose of this project was to build a simple editor for surfaces of revolution. It was one of the last projects from this class. I calculated and drew two cubic Bezier curves to create the 3D object. There are 6 points along these two curves, two of which are control points which the user can click and drag to manipulate the shape of the object. There are 20 sample points along each Bezier curve segment. The two curves are also C0 continuous at the meeting point. In order to do the rotation, I calculated the vertices for the surface of revolution by rotating the Bezier curve around the axis of symmetry in steps of 10 degrees. The vertices are connected in quad shapes to form the surface along with calculated normals. There is also a directional light source that illuminates the object.

I also put a texture on the surface of revolution which stretched across the entire surface area from top to bottom . This involved doing texture mapping where I calculated the texture coordinates for each vertex and made sure the mapping was right side up. If you press the 'r' key, you can toggle between rotation and edit mode, the first being able to rotate the object and the other being able to edit the object with the control points. If you press the 't' key, you can toggle between the object with and without the texture.
