GCube: Grocery Shopping Game

Date: Fall 2015

As a parent, shopping for groceries with hyperactive, restless children can be tiring and distracting. GCube leverages the curiosity and energy of children to help parents find groceries while staying entertained. This also makes grocery shopping a fun bonding experience between parent and child.

The project was part of an HCI class at Stanford with a group of two other people, a Mechanical Engineering Masters student and a Learning, Design, and Technology Masters student. I worked on the mobile web application that "paired" with the board using HTML/CSS, Javascript, node.js, and express. I also worked on creating the prototypes, user need finding, and the logo with Adobe Illustrator.



Step 1 Enter your grocery list through the mobile web app which will send the information to the board.
Step 2 Children will help parents find the grocery items on the board. Grocery items will have the corresponding cubes that children will match on the board. In our last iteration, the cubes were replaced with a QR scan.
Step 3 Your child wins the game and you have your groceries along with a memoriable and fun experience to share.
